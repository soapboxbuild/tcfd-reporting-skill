# TCFD Climate Disclosure for Real Estate

## Framework Status — Read First

**TCFD was superseded by ISSB IFRS S2** (effective 2024 for most jurisdictions).

ISSB S2 is **mandatory** in:
- Australia: from FY2025 (AASB)
- UK: transitioning from TCFD mandate
- Canada: CSA guidance aligned with ISSB S2
- Singapore: SGX mandatory from FY2023 (large caps)
- Japan: FSA mandatory from FY2023 (prime market)
- EU: CSRD/ESRS E1 (parallel framework, separate structure)

**For entities in mandatory-ISSB jurisdictions:** Structure the disclosure under ISSB S2. Note TCFD as the conceptual predecessor. ISSB S2 requires QUANTIFIED financial impact estimates — qualitative scenario descriptions alone are non-compliant.

**Minimum scenario set:**
- One below 2°C: IPCC SSP1-2.6 or IEA NZE 2050
- One above 3°C: IPCC SSP5-8.5 or IEA STEPS (Current Policies)

**For voluntary disclosures:** TCFD four-pillar structure (Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, Metrics & Targets) remains the appropriate framework.

## TCFD Framework: Four Pillars

### 1. Governance
- Board oversight of climate risks and opportunities
- Management's role in assessing and managing climate risks
- ESG committee structure, reporting cadence

### 2. Strategy
**Identify climate risks and opportunities over:**
- Short term (0-3 years): near-term physical events, regulatory changes
- Medium term (3-10 years): BEPS compliance waves, energy price risk
- Long term (10+ years): chronic physical risk, stranded assets

**Scenario Analysis (minimum 2 scenarios):**
- <2°C (Paris-aligned / RCP 2.6): transition risk dominant — carbon pricing, efficiency mandates, stranded assets
- >3°C (Business as usual / RCP 8.5): physical risk dominant — flooding, heat stress, sea level rise

### 3. Risk Management
- Climate risk identification process (integration with ERM)
- Risk prioritization methodology
- Integration into investment/asset management decisions

### 4. Metrics and Targets
**Required metrics for real estate:**
- Scope 1 GHG (tCO2e): direct combustion on-site
- Scope 2 GHG (tCO2e): purchased electricity, heat, cooling
- Scope 3 Category 13 (tCO2e): downstream leased assets (tenant energy)
- Scope 3 Category 15 (tCO2e): investments (for asset managers)
- Energy intensity (kWh/m²)
- % portfolio with green building certification
- % portfolio in high physical risk zones

## Physical Risk Assessment

### Acute Risks (event-driven)
- Flooding: FEMA flood maps, EU Floods Directive zones, First Street Foundation
- Wildfire: CAL FIRE, USFS risk maps
- Hurricane/Wind: NOAA wind speed maps, coastal exposure
- Extreme heat: NOAA, EU Copernicus heat days >35°C

### Chronic Risks (long-term shifts)
- Sea level rise: NOAA 2050/2100 projections by location
- Chronic heat: cooling degree days increase, HVAC demand
- Water stress: WRI Aqueduct water risk atlas
- Permafrost/subsidence: regional geology maps

### Risk Scoring Approach
For each asset: score 1-5 on each hazard × exposure × sensitivity × adaptive capacity
Aggregate to portfolio-level heatmap

**Physical risk data:** Invoke Continuuiti or Moody's RMS MCP for property-level physical risk scores if available. Report current score, 2030 projection, 2050 projection under both 2°C and 4°C warming.

## Transition Risk Assessment

### Policy & Legal
- Carbon pricing exposure (EU ETS, RGGI, BC Carbon Tax)
- BEPS/LL97 fine exposure timeline
- Minimum energy efficiency standards (MEES UK, EU EPBD)

### Market
- Green premium: LEED/BREEAM certified vs. non-certified rent differential (5-15%)
- Brown discount: energy-inefficient buildings lose rental value
- Stranded asset risk (CRREM Misalignment Year)

### Technology
- Heat pump cost curve (falling, grid decarbonization pace)
- On-site solar/storage economics
- EV charging infrastructure requirements

## SEC Climate Rules (US)
- Scope 1+2 disclosure mandatory for large accelerated filers
- Scope 3 if material or part of targets
- Climate-related risk materiality assessment required
- Third-party assurance required for Scope 1+2 (phased)

## ISSB Standards (IFRS S2)
- Global baseline for climate disclosure (effective Jan 2024)
- Cross-industry metric categories
- Adopted/referencing: UK, EU, Canada, Australia, Singapore, Japan
